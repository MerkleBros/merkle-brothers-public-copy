What is Merkle Bros?

Merkle Bros is a trading card game hosted on Ethereum where players can vote to manipulate the ‘meta’.

Players can introduce new cards and vote to alter the attributes of existing cards.

This ‘governance’ system will allow the community to self-regulate, diminishing the strength of ‘overpowered’ cards, strengthening cards that are too weak, and introducing entirely new cards.

In this way community governance can allow for an ever-evolving meta where fresh strategies are forced to emerge.

Metamask (or another tool for interacting with Ethereum) is required to buy cards or prints.

Where's the game?

Merkle Bros is in the early stages of development. The main card game doesn’t exist yet.

Currently players can collect cards to complete sets and acquire ‘totems'. Totems allow players to generate more cards for free.

This ‘collection game’ is designed to reward early players and to raise money for development.

You can also purchase limited edition prints (real, physical, blockchain tracked prints!) if you are interested in supporting the game or if you think the art is pretty neat.

What is a card?

Cards in Merkle Bros are represented as ‘tokens’.

These tokens are created by the Merkle Bros smart contract(link to smart contract). The smart contract tracks ownership of cards by associating an ethereum wallet address with a given card.

The Ethereum network is used to track ownership of cards and to call governance votes, but gameplay will occur on merklebros.com.

How do I get cards?

Cards are acquired by buying packs.

Players may choose the number of cards in a pack. Players can also purchase promo cards and prints to help support game development.

Cards are generated randomly in packs with the following rarities:

Common - 50% of cards are common

Rare - 35% of cards are rare

Epic - 14% of cards are epic

Legendary - 1% of cards are legendary

Promo cards and prints will also be given away to social media followers periodically in giveaways. Follow us on Twitter and Tumblr for a chance to win.

Metamask (or another tool for interacting with Ethereum) is required to buy cards or prints.

How does governance work?

Players will be able to vote on card attributes of existing cards (attack, health, cost to play, card actions, values of card actions) and vote to introduce new cards to the game.

Votes will be weighted by card count.

Votes will be tracked and enforced by a ‘governance’ smart contract hosted on the Ethereum network.

More technicals on the cards?

Card instances are tokens owned by the player. These are generated randomly when players buy packs (or non-randomly if a player buys a promo card). Each instance has an associated ‘prototype’ that identifies the card. Each prototype represents a card blueprint that has been introduced into the game (either by the Merkle Bros team or by a community governance vote). Prototypes have the following attributes:

Prototype Id - A unique identifying number for this card blueprint

Set - The set this card belongs to

Rarity - Card rarity (common, rare, epic, or legendary)

Action Point Cost - Cost to play this card

Attack - Attack value (if card is a combatant)

Health - Health value (if card is a combatant)

Action One - Action that occurs when playing card

Action Two - There can be up to three unique actions when playing a card

Action Three

Action One Value - Value of the associated action that occurs when playing a card

Action Two Value

Action Three Value

Title - Name of the card

Artist - Artist associated with the card illustration

Community governance vote can modify the following attributes of a card prototype: action point cost to play, attack value, health value, card actions that occur when playing card, values associated with the actions that occur when playing card. Card title, artist, rarity, and set cannot be altered once created. Community governance can also vote to introduce new card prototypes to the game.

The card game will acquire card data directly from the smart contract.

Feel free to read the smart contract if you are programming inclined (link to smart contract).

What is the 'collection game'?

Currently players can begin collecting cards and completing sets.

When players complete a set, they can claim a ‘totem’ representing that set.

A totem allows the player to generate a random card from every set that has a shared rarity.

So completing a common set would unlock a common totem which can be used to generate a random card from every common set.

The collection game is intended to reward early players and to raise money for game development.

As new cards and sets are introduced, it will become harder for players to complete sets and unlock totems. Early players are rewarded with additional cards by completing sets while there are still a relatively small number of cards.

What is a set?

Cards are grouped by theme into ‘sets’.

For instance, the set ‘Drawings of Happy the Herbalist’ is a collection of happy looking drawings by a mad herbalist named Happy.

Each set also has a shared rarity. So there is a ‘Drawings of Happy the Herbalist - Common’ set, as well as rare and epic sets.

Legendary sets do not exist, as each legendary card is a one-off card.

What is a totem?

A totem is an unlockable card that players get after completing a set.

The totem matches the rarity of the set that was completed.

So completing a ‘rare’ set would unlock a ‘rare’ totem representing that set.

Totems can be used to generate a random card from every set that has a matching rarity.

So a ‘rare’ totem can generate a random card from every ‘rare’ set, including rare sets released in the future.

What is the setting?

The game is set in a fantasy world where Ethereum crystals are real! The crystals are held in the sky by ancient wizards. Every now and then the wizards let some of these magical crystals fall to the ground.

Individuals and civilizations use and study these crystals to become powerful.

There’s a capital city ruled by skeletons and their skeleton king.

There’s the far off icy wilderness of Elias.

The forest where Happy the Herbalist lures unlucky travelers.

There's the Royal Ladies who hold court over the elements.

There’s various monstrosities and cute creatures as well. And a land where it’s always night. And more!

What are the limited edition prints?

Merkle Bros is offering physical, blockchain tracked prints of our favorite illustrations.

These will be printed in a limited edition run of 10 copies per illustration.

Ownership and authenticity of these fine art prints will be tracked on the Ethereum blockchain via smart contract.

Purchasers of fine art prints will be required to submit a name and mailing address.

Prints will be manufactured and shipped in Summer 2018.

Print dimensions and other details (paper stock, ink choice, etc) to be determined at time of manufacture based on quality and cost constraints.

How is print ownership tracked?

The smart contract tracks each Ethereum wallet that purchases a fine art print and mints a token for each purchase. In this way art 'cards' are tracked the same way as regular 'cards' by associating wallet addresses to token Ids.

The physical print itself will come with an NFC chip containing a Certificate of Authenticity and metadata about the artwork.

We are tentatively planning to use CoA's from the Ethereum Art Collective.
